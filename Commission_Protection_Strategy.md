## What Happens If Owner Tries to Circumvent?

### Scenario 1: Owner Contacts Tenant Directly

**Detection:**
- Tenant reports it (per confirmation email)
- Or you notice lack of platform communication

**Your Response:**
1. Email owner: "I noticed you contacted [Tenant] directly. Per our agreement section 3.4, all initial contact must go through platform. Tracking ID: [ID]. Commission still applies."
2. Document the violation
3. If they proceed to rent: Invoice with tracking ID + agreement reference

**Legal Standing:** Strong (you have tracking ID + agreement)

---

### Scenario 2: Owner Removes Property Then Rents to Your Lead

**Detection:**
- Owner removes property from platform
- Later you find out they rented it
- Tenant might even tell you

**Your Response:**
1. Check your records for tracking ID
2. Email: "Per agreement section 2.4, commission applies within 12 months of referral. [Tenant Name] was referred on [Date] with tracking ID [ID]. Invoice attached."
3. Send formal invoice
4. If unpaid: Legal action with your documentation

**Legal Standing:** Very Strong (12-month clause in signed agreement)

---

### Scenario 3: Owner Claims Tenant "Found Them Another Way"

**Detection:**
- Owner says "Oh they found me on Craigslist, not through you"

**Your Response:**
1. Pull up tracking ID and timestamp
2. Show PDF with owner's acknowledgment
3. Email: "Per our records, [Tenant Name] submitted application through platform on [Date/Time] with tracking ID [ID]. Your email acknowledging receipt is attached. Commission applies per agreement section 3.3."

**Legal Standing:** Very Strong (timestamp, tracking ID, email trail)

---

### Scenario 4: Owner Delays/Refuses Payment

**Detection:**
- You invoiced, they ignore or refuse

**Your Response:**
1. Send formal demand letter referencing agreement section 4.3
2. Note late fees (1.5% monthly interest)
3. Small claims court if under $10K (varies by state)
4. Attorney if larger amount

**Legal Standing:** Very Strong (signed agreement, tracking documentation)

## Additional Protection Tactics

### Tactic 1: Require Deposit/Fee from Owner

**How It Works:**
- Charge owner $50-100 listing fee upfront
- Or require small monthly fee ($20/month)
- Refundable if they don't get leads

**Why It Works:**
- Owner has "skin in the game"
- Less likely to circumvent someone they paid
- Covers your marketing costs
- Signals serious commitment

---

### Tactic 2: Tenant Application Fee

**How It Works:**
- Charge tenant $25-50 non-refundable application fee
- Applied to first month rent if they rent
- Paid through platform (Stripe, PayPal)

**Why It Works:**
- Tenant is now YOUR customer (not just owner's)
- Financial transaction proves your involvement
- Harder for owner to claim you weren't involved
- Generates revenue even if no rental

---

### Tactic 3: Exclusive Listing Period

**How It Works:**
- Owner must list ONLY with you for 30-60 days
- Or if non-exclusive, right of first referral

**Why It Works:**
- Prevents owner from "testing" other platforms simultaneously
- Gives you clear timeframe
- If rented during exclusivity, definitely your lead

---

### Tactic 4: Require Owner to Report Outcomes

**How It Works:**
- Agreement requires owner to notify you within 24-48 hours when:
  - They rent to ANYONE
  - They reject your applicant
  - Property is off market

**Why It Works:**
- Creates obligation to communicate
- Catch circumvention early
- Shows you're monitoring

---

### Tactic 5: Tenant Reviews/Follow-Up

**How It Works:**
- 30 days after application, email tenant:
  - "Did you find housing?"
  - "Did you rent from [Owner]?"
  - "If so, was it smooth?"

**Why It Works:**
- Tenant will tell you if they rented from your lead
- Catches owners who didn't report
- Shows you're tracking outcomes

If owner refuses to pay commission:

### Step 2: Formal Notice (Day 8-14)
```
Subject: PAST DUE - Invoice #[NUM]

Hi [Owner],

Invoice #[NUM] for $[AMOUNT] is now past due. Per our agreement
section 4.3, late fees apply at 1.5% monthly.

Please remit payment by [Date] to avoid additional fees.

Documentation:
- Tracking ID: [ID]
- Application Date: [Date]
- Signed Agreement: [Date]
```

### Step 3: Demand Letter (Day 15-30)
```
Formal letter from you (or attorney) demanding payment, citing:
- Signed agreement
- Tracking documentation
- Email records
- 10 days to pay or legal action
```

### Step 4: Small Claims Court (Day 31+)

**If Under $10K (varies by state):**
- File small claims court case
- Costs: $30-100 filing fee
- Bring: Agreement, tracking records, emails, invoice
- Usually decided in 30-60 days
- High win rate with good documentation

**If Over $10K:**
- Hire attorney
- Send demand letter
- File lawsuit if needed
- Attorney fees recoverable per agreement section 9.4

## Questions to Consider

Before launching with multiple owners:

1. **Do I need a real estate broker license in my state?**
   - Some states require it for commission-based rental services
   - Consult local real estate attorney

2. **Should I use an LLC or personal name?**
   - LLC protects personal assets
   - More professional
   - Required for scaling

3. **How will I enforce internationally?**
   - If owners are in India, harder to enforce
   - Require US-based owners only, or
   - Higher deposit from international owners

4. **What's my audit process?**
   - Monthly check-ins with owners?
   - Automated tenant follow-ups?
   - Public records search?

5. **Insurance needed?**
   - Errors & omissions insurance?
   - General liability?
